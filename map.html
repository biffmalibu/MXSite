Quincy Market

Quincy Market is an ideal destination to spend quality time with your loved ones and savor delicious food. The marketplace boasts an impressive lineup of 18 restaurants and 35 colonnade eateries, catering to a diverse range of cuisines. With such a wide selection, there's bound to be something that suits your palate. Visitors often wonder what quintessential Bostonian dish or delicacy they should try at Quincy Market. Some of the all-time favorites that you must sample include:

  • Clam Chowder
  • Boston cream pie - official dessert of Massachusetts
  • Bostonian baked beans with brown bread
  • Lobster rolls
  • Seafood (lobster, whole belly clams)

Eataly

Eataly is a renowned Italian marketplace, which is dedicated to promoting affordable, high-quality regional Italian food with a local twist. It holds the distinction of being the largest Italian marketplace worldwide. Eataly USA currently encompasses six locations, namely Eataly New York Flatiron, Eataly Chicago, Eataly NYC Downtown, Eataly Boston, Eataly L.A., and Eataly Las Vegas. Each of these locations is modeled after the first Eataly store, founded by Oscar Farinetti in Turin, Italy, in 2007.

Eataly Boston, in particular, is an authentic three-story Italian experience, where visitors can indulge in a range of culinary delights, shop for premium products and enhance their knowledge about Italian cuisine. The marketplace features four full-service restaurants, various retail counters (such as meat, seafood, fresh pasta, pastry and chocolate, cheese and cured meats), two coffee bars, a bakery, a salad bar, a mozzarella lab, a gelato counter, and all the essential grocery departments, including sweets, spreads and jams, hundreds of olive oils, and dry pastas (sourced from both Italy and New England). Additionally, Eataly Boston also boasts a housewares section, a book store, a cooking school, numerous private event spaces, and much more!

Boston Public Market

The Boston Public Market is a spacious, year-round indoor marketplace that brings together approximately 30 New England-based artisans and food producers under one roof. Here, visitors can find a wide variety of fresh foods, specialty items, crafts, and prepared meals. The marketplace prides itself on sourcing seasonal, locally grown and produced goods from Massachusetts and other New England states. The Market's offerings include an array of fresh produce, meat and poultry, eggs, dairy, seafood, baked goods, specialty items, as well as a range of breakfast, lunch, and dinner options. It's worth noting that everything sold at the Boston Public Market is either produced or sourced from New England, depending on the season. So, whether you're a local or a visitor to Boston, you're sure to find something to your liking and can shop with the knowledge that you're supporting the region's farmers and artisans.
