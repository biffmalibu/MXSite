Dangers

Death/Disappearance


- The International Organization for Migration documented 686 deaths and disappearances of migrants on the US-Mexico border in 2022


- 307 of the deaths on the border were linked to the crossing of the Sonoran and Chihuahuan Deserts. At least 212 people died in the Sahara Desert in 2022


Migration Patterns


- Mexican immigration to the US saw its peak in the 80's and 90's, when the Mexican economy was more unstable


- Since then, the economy has improved and the number of Mexican immigrants has steadily decreased since 2007.


- The 2008 financial crisis, stricter immigration enforcement and reduced birth rates have likely contributed to this decline
