Newbury Street

Newbury Street is a charming, mile-long thoroughfare that runs east of Massachusetts Avenue in Boston. It's a well-known destination for both locals and tourists, lined with hundreds of shops and restaurants housed within historic 19th-century brownstones. The most upscale boutiques are clustered near the Boston Public Garden end of the street. However, as you head towards Mass Ave, the shops become slightly less expensive and more bohemian. To the west of Mass Ave, Newbury Street is adjacent to the Mass Pike on its unbuilt southern side, while the northern side mostly comprises parking and rear service areas for buildings on Commonwealth Avenue. The street is briefly interrupted by the Muddy River before continuing its path to abut the Pike until it reaches Brookline Avenue. There's currently a proposed plan to deck over the Pike to the west of Mass Ave, which could pave the way for an expansion of the shopping district.

Copley Place

Copley Place is an upscale shopping destination situated in Boston's historic Back Bay neighborhood, located at 100 Huntington Ave, Boston MA 02116. The center boasts 75 outstanding stores, including top-notch brands like Neiman Marcus, Barneys New York, Tiffany & Co., Jimmy Choo, Intimacy, Tourneau, Salvatore Ferragamo, David Yurman, A|X Armani Exchange, Louis Vuitton, Emporio Armani, Elie Tahari, Christian Dior, Burberry, and BCBGMAXAZRIA. Spread over a 9.5-acre site, Copley Place offers two levels of shopping, a variety of restaurants, four office buildings, 1,400 parking spaces, and two hotels - The Westin Hotel and The Boston Marriott Copley Place. Whether you're in search of high-end fashion, jewelry, or accessories, or are simply looking to indulge in some retail therapy, Copley Place is a one-stop destination that caters to all your needs.

The Shops at Prudential Center

The Prudential Center, a premier shopping destination in the Northeast, boasts more than 75 upscale retail establishments. Visitors can indulge in retail therapy at high-end stores such as Barnes & Noble, Saks Fifth Avenue, Lacoste, Lord & Taylor, and Club Monaco. In addition to its impressive collection of stores, the mall offers a delightful food court where shoppers can grab a quick bite, as well as several sit-down restaurants including Legal Seafood and the Cheesecake Factory. Moreover, the mall is connected to Copley Place, the Sheraton, and the Mandarin Oriental, allowing visitors to explore a large part of the Back Bay without stepping out of the enclosed space. For breathtaking views, visitors can head to the Prudential Tower's Skywalk Observatory, which provides a stunning panoramic view of the city and beyond. Whether you're in search of high-end fashion or a relaxing dining experience, the Prudential Center has everything to offer.

Haymarket Outdoor Market

Located in the heart of Bostonâ€™s historic downtown, Haymarket is an iconic open-air market that has been operating for nearly 300 years. This cultural cornerstone of the city is home to over 40 independent vendors, who continue the tradition of selling fresh fruits, vegetables, and seafood at affordable prices. Visitors can connect with the history and culture of Boston while enjoying the sights and sounds of one of the oldest markets in America.

Located just footsteps away from Faneuil Hall and the North End, Haymarket is the perfect place to explore the city and experience the vibrant atmosphere of Boston. In addition to the market, visitors can also enjoy historic pubs, restaurants, and ethnic groceries in the area. Whether you're a local or a tourist, Haymarket is a must-visit destination that offers a truly unique and authentic Boston experience.

The Mall at Chestnut Hill

The Mall at Chestnut Hill is located at 199 Boylston Street, Chestnut Hill, MA 02467. It is an upscale shopping destination that offers a mix of high-end boutiques and luxury department stores, as well as a variety of restaurants and cafes. In addition to the stores mentioned above, it also features Brooks Brothers, J.Crew, Michael Kors, Sephora, and Williams-Sonoma. The mall is anchored by Bloomingdale's and has two levels of shopping, with a bright and airy interior design. With ample parking and easy access to public transportation, it is a convenient destination for shoppers from all over the Boston area.
